Title: Northstar CRM Pricing and Billing
Last updated: 2026-07-08
Owner: Billing Support

Northstar CRM offers three standard subscription plans: Starter, Pro, and Business. Pricing in this training knowledge base is intentionally realistic but fictional. Students should treat this document as the source of truth for billing answers in the capstone project.

Starter plan:

- Best for solo users and very small teams.
- Includes contact and company management.
- Includes one sales pipeline.
- Includes manual task management and basic reminders.
- Includes CSV import up to 5,000 rows per import.
- Includes basic activity timeline.
- Includes email support with a two-business-day target response.
- Does not include custom fields, custom dashboards, advanced permissions, or priority support.

Pro plan:

- Best for growing sales teams that need automation and reporting.
- Includes everything in Starter.
- Includes up to five customizable sales pipelines.
- Includes custom fields for contacts, companies, and deals.
- Includes Google Workspace and Microsoft 365 email sync.
- Includes saved views and team dashboards.
- Includes duplicate detection and assisted merge.
- Includes workflow reminders for follow-up tasks.
- Includes role-based permissions for Admin, Manager, and Member.
- Includes email and chat support with a one-business-day target response.

Business plan:

- Best for larger teams with stricter operational requirements.
- Includes everything in Pro.
- Includes unlimited customizable pipelines.
- Includes advanced permissions and team workspaces.
- Includes audit log export.
- Includes SSO configuration support.
- Includes sandbox workspace for testing imports and configuration changes.
- Includes priority support with a four-business-hour target response.
- Includes quarterly success review for annual contracts.

Billing cycles:

- Customers can choose monthly or annual billing.
- Annual billing is charged once per year at the start of the subscription term.
- Monthly billing renews on the same calendar day each month when possible.
- Seat changes are prorated for the active billing period.
- Removing a user stops future charges for that seat but does not automatically delete CRM data owned by that user.

Invoices and receipts:

- Account admins can download invoices from Settings > Billing > Invoices.
- Billing receipts are emailed to the billing contact after successful payment.
- If the billing contact changes, past receipts are not automatically resent. An admin can download them from Billing settings.

Failed payments:

- When payment fails, Northstar CRM retries the card automatically.
- Admins see a billing banner in the workspace.
- If payment is not resolved within the grace period, the workspace may become read-only.
- Read-only mode allows viewing and exporting data but blocks new records, imports, and workflow changes.

Refund and duplicate-charge policy:

- The support assistant must not promise a refund or credit.
- Suspected duplicate charges must be escalated to Billing Support.
- If a customer says "I was charged twice this month," treat it as a suspected duplicate charge and escalate to Billing Support.
- The assistant can ask for non-sensitive details such as invoice number, billing email, approximate charge date, and the last four digits of the card.
- The assistant must not ask for full card number, CVV, full bank account number, or account password.
- Billing Support investigates duplicate-charge reports and responds according to the customer support plan.

Plan-change guidance:

- Customers can upgrade from Starter to Pro or Business from Settings > Billing > Plan.
- Downgrades may remove access to features such as custom fields, additional pipelines, advanced reports, or audit exports.
- Customers should export important reports before downgrading.
- If a customer asks whether they should downgrade, explain the feature tradeoffs and recommend contacting Billing Support for account-specific pricing.

Escalation triggers:

- Duplicate charge or unexpected charge.
- Refund request.
- Invoice dispute.
- Failed payment on a time-sensitive renewal.
- Customer cannot access billing settings.
- Billing contact is no longer with the company.
