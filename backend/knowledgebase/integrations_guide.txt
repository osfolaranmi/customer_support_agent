Title: Northstar CRM Integrations Guide
Last updated: 2026-07-08
Owner: Integrations Support

Northstar CRM supports selected integrations for email, calendar activity, web forms, CSV imports, and third-party automation platforms. Integrations help customers reduce manual data entry and keep sales activity visible in the CRM.

Supported email integrations:

- Google Workspace Gmail.
- Microsoft 365 Outlook.

Email sync features:

- Log sent and received emails on matching contact timelines.
- Track email subject, sender, recipient, timestamp, and message body when sync permissions allow.
- Create contacts from email recipients when the user enables contact creation.
- Exclude private domains and specific email addresses from sync.

Connecting Gmail:

1. Go to Settings > Integrations.
2. Select Google Workspace.
3. Choose "Connect account."
4. Sign in with the Google account used for sales email.
5. Review the permissions screen.
6. Approve access.
7. Choose sync preferences:
   - Log emails for existing contacts only.
   - Create new contacts from email recipients.
   - Exclude personal domains.
8. Save settings.

Connecting Microsoft 365:

1. Go to Settings > Integrations.
2. Select Microsoft 365.
3. Choose "Connect account."
4. Sign in with the Microsoft account used for sales email.
5. Approve the requested permissions.
6. Choose sync preferences.
7. Save settings.

Email sync delays:

- Initial sync can take 10 to 30 minutes depending on mailbox size.
- New messages usually appear within five minutes.
- If messages do not appear after 30 minutes, reconnect the integration and check sync filters.

Calendar activity:

- Calendar activity can be logged when the connected email provider grants calendar permissions.
- Northstar CRM does not create meetings directly from CRM records in the Starter plan.
- Pro and Business customers can associate meetings with contacts and deals.

Web forms:

- Web forms can create contacts from lead capture pages.
- Forms can map fields to standard and custom contact fields.
- Form submissions appear on contact timelines.
- Duplicate detection may suggest merging if the email already exists.

Third-party automation:

- Business plan customers can use webhook-based workflows for supported events.
- Webhook payloads include record ID, event type, timestamp, and changed fields.
- Webhooks should be tested in a sandbox workspace before production use.

Integration troubleshooting:

- If an integration disconnects, the user should reconnect from Settings > Integrations.
- If email sync logs too many personal messages, update excluded domains and sync rules.
- If a connected account belongs to a former employee, an Admin should disconnect it from Settings > Team or Settings > Integrations.
- If the customer sees an OAuth permission error, ask them to confirm that their Google or Microsoft admin allows third-party app access.

Escalation triggers:

- OAuth approval fails for all users in the workspace.
- Sync duplicates large numbers of records.
- Connected email belongs to a former employee and cannot be removed.
- Customer reports private messages appearing where they should not.
- Integration outage affects a time-sensitive sales workflow.
