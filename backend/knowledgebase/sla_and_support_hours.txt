Title: Northstar CRM SLA and Support Hours
Last updated: 2026-07-08
Owner: Support Operations

Northstar CRM provides support based on subscription plan and issue priority. This document defines target response times for the capstone project.

Support channels:

- Help center articles are available at all times.
- Email support is available to all plans.
- Chat support is available to Pro and Business customers.
- Priority support is available to Business customers.

Standard support hours:

- Monday through Friday.
- 8:00 AM to 6:00 PM Eastern Time.
- Excludes major US holidays.

Starter plan targets:

- Email support.
- Two-business-day target first response.
- No guaranteed chat support.
- No priority queue.

Pro plan targets:

- Email and chat support.
- One-business-day target first response.
- Faster handling for high-priority technical issues when business impact is clear.

Business plan targets:

- Email, chat, and priority support.
- Four-business-hour target first response for priority issues.
- P1 issues are routed to the urgent queue.
- Quarterly success review for annual Business contracts.

Priority handling:

P1 Critical:
- Target first response: as soon as possible during support hours.
- Assigned to Security Response, Technical Support, or Billing Support depending on issue type.
- Examples: suspected unauthorized access, complete workspace outage, multiple users cannot log in, urgent business event blocked.

P2 High:
- Target first response: same business day for Pro and Business when possible.
- Examples: duplicate charge, email sync failure affecting a team, import problem affecting more than 10,000 records.

P3 Normal:
- Target first response follows plan-level target.
- Examples: non-urgent integration issue, report mismatch, permission question.

P4 Low:
- Target first response follows plan-level target.
- Examples: how-to question, feature request, minor visual issue.

Important boundaries:

- Response targets are not guaranteed resolution times.
- The assistant must not promise exact resolution time.
- The assistant must not promise compensation, refund, credit, or SLA remedy.
- The assistant can tell the customer which team should review the issue and what information to include.

After-hours guidance:

- The assistant can still provide safe self-service steps.
- Urgent security, outage, or billing issues should be escalated with priority.
- The assistant should explain that support response timing may depend on plan level and support hours.

Customer-facing wording:

"I can help collect the right details and route this to the correct support team. I cannot promise a specific resolution time, but based on the information you shared this should be treated as [priority] and assigned to [team]."
